Societal implications

Photography has a way of capturing and presenting cultural truths that are often easy to ignore. For Columbia, this meant exposing issues of racial segregation and poverty that many residents didn't think were problems in the community.

David Rees, the current faculty chair for the Missouri School of Journalism photojournalism department spoke about the race issues in Columbia brought to light by the original photography workshop.

When this picture was taken in 1949, Columbia housed many people in tenements. Much of the housing was still segregated and African Americans were largely forced to reside in the poorer parts of town.

The grocery store Lucky's Market now takes up the place the tenements used to be.
